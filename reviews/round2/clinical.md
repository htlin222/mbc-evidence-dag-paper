# Round-2 Clinical Review

**Reviewer role.** Breast medical oncologist, ESMO mBC guideline panellist. Adversarial review of Round-1 corrections, the new trial edges (postMONARCH, INAVO120, TROPiCS-02, OlympiAD), and residual clinical/encoding integrity.

**Scope.** `manuscript/main.tex`, `manuscript/discussion.tex`, `analysis/02_extract_eligibility.py`, `analysis/03_encode_guideline.py`, `analysis/05_compute_efdpr.py`, `analysis/06_compute_odi.py`, `data/processed/trials_structured.json`, `data/processed/esmo_decision_tree.json`, `data/results/efdpr.json`, raw payload `data/raw/ctgov_trials.json`.

Round 1 fixed two specific trial-identity bugs (postMONARCH and INAVO120) and added two new edges (TROPiCS-02, OlympiAD). The good news: those four corrections are themselves clean (sections F–G below). The bad news: Round 1 ask #12 — "verify every NCT-to-name binding in `STRUCTURED`" — has not been addressed, and the raw ClinicalTrials.gov payload exposes **four further trial-identity errors in the 12 trials that Round 1 did not focus on**, plus three additional clinical/encoding issues that survived Round 1 unchallenged. Some of these errors put a non-HR+/HER2- trial in the corpus (monarcHER is HER2-POSITIVE disease), which is more serious than the postMONARCH error fixed last round.

I keep my Round 1 numbering style: each ask is a single concrete request to the authors.

## A. Trial-identity errors that Round 1 missed and Round 2 did not repair

These are verifiable against the briefTitle/acronym fields that are already cached in `data/raw/ctgov_trials.json` — no external lookup required. The Round 1 reviewer specifically asked for this audit (ask #3) and it appears not to have been performed.

1. **NCT02513394 is PALLAS, not PALOMA-2.** `analysis/02_extract_eligibility.py:113-121` and `data/processed/trials_structured.json:193-213` label NCT02513394 as "PALOMA-2" (claimed 1L CDK4/6i+AI in metastatic disease). The raw payload (`data/raw/ctgov_trials.json`, `acronym: "PALLAS"`, officialTitle "PALbociclib CoLlaborative Adjuvant Study... A Randomized Phase III Trial of Palbociclib With Standard Adjuvant Endocrine Therapy ... for ... Early Breast Cancer") is PALLAS, the **adjuvant** palbociclib trial. PALLAS does not enrol metastatic patients at all and cannot support any node in this manuscript. The real PALOMA-2 is **NCT01740427**. **Action:** swap to NCT01740427, re-pull the raw record, re-extract, and re-run the pipeline. The bug is identical in shape to the postMONARCH/INAVO120 errors fixed in Round 1 — wrong NCT, right name in code. As-encoded, the first-line node G1 is supported by a 4-edge bundle that includes an adjuvant-disease trial.

2. **NCT02675231 is monarcHER (HR+/HER2-POSITIVE), not MONARCH-3.** `analysis/02_extract_eligibility.py:122-130` and `trials_structured.json:215-233` label NCT02675231 as "MONARCH-3". The raw payload acronym is "monarcHER", and the briefTitle says "in Women With HR+, **HER2+** Locally Advanced or Metastatic Breast Cancer". monarcHER is a Phase 2 HER2-positive trial (abemaciclib + trastuzumab ± fulvestrant vs chemotherapy + trastuzumab) — it is out of scope for an HR+/HER2-negative manuscript and must not appear in the corpus at all. The real MONARCH-3 is **NCT02246621**, which the manuscript currently mislabels as MONALEESA-3 (see ask 3). **Action:** remove NCT02675231 from the corpus entirely, replace its slot in G1's `cited_trials` with NCT02246621 (the real MONARCH-3) once that record's labelling is fixed. This is the highest-severity clinical error in Round 2 because it puts a HER2+ trial inside an HR+/HER2- analysis.

3. **NCT02246621 is MONARCH-3, not MONALEESA-3.** `02_extract_eligibility.py:100-112` labels NCT02246621 as "MONALEESA-3". The raw acronym is "MONARCH 3", and the officialTitle ("...Anastrozole or Letrozole... Plus LY2835219... in Postmenopausal Women With Hormone Receptor-Positive, HER2-Negative Locoregionally Recurrent or Metastatic Breast Cancer With No Prior Systemic Therapy in This Disease Setting") is the classic MONARCH-3 protocol — 1L abemaciclib + NSAI in endocrine-naive postmenopausal HR+/HER2- mBC. The real MONALEESA-3 (ribociclib + fulvestrant in postmenopausal women, 1L and 2L strata) is **NCT02422615**, which is absent from the corpus entirely. **Action:** (a) re-label NCT02246621 as MONARCH-3 (and move it from G3's `cited_trials` to G1's, where as a 1L endocrine-naive trial it belongs); (b) add NCT02422615 (real MONALEESA-3) to the corpus and to G1 + G3 as ESMO actually cites it.

4. **NCT02763566 is MONARCH plus, not MONALEESA-7.** `02_extract_eligibility.py:131-139` labels NCT02763566 as "MONALEESA-7" with `menopausal_status: "pre"`. The raw acronym is "MONARCH plus" and the officialTitle is a postmenopausal abemaciclib + NSAI / fulvestrant trial conducted primarily in China — there is no pre-menopausal cohort. The real MONALEESA-7 (the pre/peri-menopausal ribociclib + tamoxifen/NSAI + goserelin trial) is **NCT02278120**. **Action:** swap NCT02763566 → NCT02278120 in both `STRUCTURED` and in G1/G2 `cited_trials`. The premenopausal-CDK4/6i node G2 is currently supported by the wrong trial; under any tolerance G2 should become evidence-free unless the real MONALEESA-7 is added.

5. **Cumulative impact of asks 1–4 on the headline number.** Of the 16 trials in the corpus, **four (25%) are mislabelled**. Of these, one (monarcHER, NCT02675231) is in the wrong disease subset entirely and must be removed; the other three need NCT swaps and re-extraction from the correct primary publications. The 4-edge in-degree of G1 (`"NCT01958021", "NCT02513394", "NCT02675231", "NCT02763566"`) survives only because each of the four NCTs happens to share an "abemaciclib/palbociclib/ribociclib in CDK4/6i+endocrine 1L mBC" narrative description with the intended trial — but two of the four point at adjuvant or HER2+ studies that cannot legally support G1 under the manuscript's own state-match and biomarker-match rules. **Action:** re-run the full pipeline after asks 1–4, and report the corrected EFDPR transparently with a note that the Round 1 reviewer's request for an NCT-vs-name audit (ask #3) had been declined, then enforced in Round 2.

6. **Add a regression test that ties NCT to trial name.** A 10-line Python test (`pytest tests/test_nct_identity.py`) that loads `data/raw/ctgov_trials.json` and asserts, for each entry in `STRUCTURED`, that `payload["protocolSection"]["identificationModule"]["acronym"].lower()` equals (or contains) `STRUCTURED[nct]["trial_name"].lower().replace(" ", "")` would have caught all four errors above. Add this test and run it in CI before the v1.0.0 tag.

## B. ESMO decision-tree encoding errors that survived Round 1

7. **G10 still cites NCT03997123 (CAPItello-290, TNBC) in the encoding source file.** `analysis/03_encode_guideline.py:131` still reads `"cited_trials": ["NCT03997123"]`. The Round 1 reviewer flagged NCT03997123 in the trial corpus as the wrong NCT (it is CAPItello-290, TNBC, not postMONARCH); the fix went into `STRUCTURED` (postMONARCH became NCT05169567) but the parallel reference in the ESMO decision tree was overlooked. As-is, G10's "cited_trials" lists a trial that does not exist in the corpus (because `STRUCTURED` no longer carries NCT03997123) and which would be ineligible anyway (TNBC). **Action:** swap `NCT03997123 → NCT05169567` in `03_encode_guideline.py:131`. (The downstream `efdpr.json` happens to be correct because `supports()` reads from `STRUCTURED`-derived edges, not from `cited_trials`; but the published Supplementary Table S3 will carry the wrong NCT unless this is fixed.)

8. **G15 lists SOLAR-1 (NCT02437318) as a supporting trial in `efdpr.json`, despite SOLAR-1 being a doublet and G15 being the inavolisib triplet recommendation.** Inspect `data/results/efdpr.json:117-119` — under strict tolerance, G15's `per_node_support` is `["NCT02437318", "NCT04191499"]`. This is wrong: G15's `recommended_classes` is "PI3Ki triplet (inavolisib + CDK4/6i + fulv)" and SOLAR-1's `drug_class` is "PI3Ki + fulvestrant" (doublet). The reason both pass `_drug_match` is that `analysis/05_compute_efdpr.py:131-132` collapses any `PI3Ki…` edge against any `PI3Ki…` guideline class using a prefix-startswith rule, so alpelisib doublet erroneously credits inavolisib triplet (the same defect was raised as Round 1 ask #24). **Action:** refactor `_drug_match` to compare on a (drug-name, regimen-composition) tuple, not on a class-prefix, so that doublet ≠ triplet and alpelisib ≠ inavolisib. The headline EFDPR is currently *understated* by the fact that G15 picks up a SOLAR-1 spurious match.

9. **G3 should list MONARCH-2 (NCT02107703) as a supporting trial under strict tolerance, but `efdpr.json:29-31` shows only PALOMA-3.** Both PALOMA-3 and MONARCH-2 have `prior_state.post_endo: true`, `post_cdk46i: false`, `biomarker: HR+/HER2-`, and `drug_class: CDK4/6i + fulvestrant` — identical to G3's required state/biomarker/class. Yet only PALOMA-3 (NCT01942135) appears as supporting G3 in the strict result. This implies a state-match bug: probably the `excluded.post_chemo_metastatic_any: true` on MONARCH-2 is being interpreted as adding a state-token that breaks superset matching. **Action:** audit `_state_match` against MONARCH-2 → G3 and demonstrate why this pairing fails; if it is a bug, fix it. (As written this defect also *understates* the support set for G3, which is the opposite direction of the G15 SOLAR-1 bug — i.e. the pipeline has bidirectional defects in `supports()`.)

10. **G16 cites only OlympiAD; ESMO 2024 cites both OlympiAD AND EMBRACA at the gBRCAm node, with talazoparib as a co-recommended class.** `03_encode_guideline.py:194` reads `"cited_trials": ["NCT02000622"], # OlympiAD; EMBRACA omitted from this pilot`. The `recommended_classes` for G16 already lists both olaparib and talazoparib at grade I-A, so the schema is self-inconsistent: the recommended-class column is dual, but the cited-trial column is singular. EMBRACA is **NCT01945775** and is the registrational trial for talazoparib in gBRCAm HER2- mBC (HR+ and TNBC strata). Round 1 ask #18 specifically asked for both to be added; the manuscript adds OlympiAD only. **Action:** either (a) add NCT01945775 (EMBRACA) to the corpus and to G16's `cited_trials`, which would also let you cross-check the gBRCAm operational-definition ODI between trials; or (b) drop talazoparib from G16's `recommended_classes` and acknowledge in Discussion that the pilot does not yet cover talazoparib. Option (a) is the clinically honest choice — EMBRACA is a phase-3 pivotal trial with an FDA approval, and excluding it from a 16-trial pivotal-trial corpus reads as cherry-picked.

11. **G6 still encodes `state: "post-CDK46i"`, but ESMO 2024 cites capivasertib + fulvestrant at the post-endocrine AKT-pathway-altered position (with prior CDK4/6i permitted but not required).** This was Round 1 ask #14 and has not been re-addressed in Round 2; the manuscript continues to call G6 evidence-free on the basis of the over-narrow state encoding. The Discussion (`discussion.tex:11`) doubles down on the framing: "CAPItello-291 enrolled a mixed pre/post-CDK4/6i population with only a subgroup readout". This is correct for the *trial* but does not justify the *guideline node* state coding. ESMO recommends capivasertib + fulvestrant in adult HR+/HER2- mBC with AKT-pathway alteration after recurrence on or progression with at least one prior endocrine therapy — not "after CDK4/6i". **Action:** recode G6's `state` to `"post-endo"`; under that encoding CAPI's `prior_state` becomes a state-match and G6 moves from evidence-free to supported, lowering strict EFDPR. If the authors disagree, they need to quote the ESMO 2024 paragraph that defines the AKT-pathway recommendation as post-CDK4/6i-specific (I believe no such paragraph exists; the recommendation is post-endo).

12. **G7's grade is still "II-B" and BYLieve (NCT03056755) is still missing.** Round 1 ask #15 noted that BYLieve is the trial ESMO cites for the post-CDK4/6i PIK3CAmut extension of alpelisib + fulvestrant. The manuscript continues to use SOLAR-1 as the sole citation, and continues to use the "composition-only" framing in Discussion. BYLieve is a phase-2 single-arm trial (not phase 3), which is a legitimate reason to leave it out of a *pivotal-phase-3* corpus, but the manuscript must say so explicitly in Methods §2.1 (which only excludes "phase 1 trials and trials without HR+/HER2- enrolment"). Right now the reader cannot tell whether BYLieve was omitted by deliberate scope choice or oversight. **Action:** add a one-sentence scope clause in Methods §2.1 that excludes single-arm phase-2 expansion cohorts, and acknowledge BYLieve by name in Discussion as a clinically-relevant non-included trial. Otherwise the G7 "evidence-free" claim is rhetorically over-loaded.

## C. Operationalization-discordance (ODI) tokens

13. **The ODI for `prior_CDK4_6i` is over-counted because two of the seven contributing trials are wrongly identified.** `06_compute_odi.py:48-56` lists tokens for EMERALD, CAPItello-291, PALOMA-3, MONARCH-2, DESTINY-Breast06, postMONARCH, and INAVO120. Of those, INAVO120's token is now `endo_resistance_window_12mo` (which is fine for the corrected NCT04191499), and postMONARCH's is `prior_CDK4_6i_required` (correct for NCT05169567). But the token-set for the prior-CDK4/6i variable should arguably also include the **adjuvant CDK4/6i exposure** that DESTINY-Breast06 path (a) explicitly accepts (1L ET+CDK4/6i in mBC, with progression on or within 6 months) — that is a finer-grained distinction than "post-endo required" and is what differentiates DB-06 from DB-04. **Action:** add a `prior_CDK4_6i_within_6mo` token to DESTINY-Breast06's set, and re-compute. (The headline ODI of 0.91 is likely robust to this; the point is that the token vocabulary in `06_compute_odi.py:23-28` should be richer than it is now.)

14. **AKT-pathway ODI uses SOLAR-1 with the singleton token `{"PIK3CAmut"}` as a comparator.** `06_compute_odi.py:43-47` is a conceptual workaround: SOLAR-1 did not enrol an "AKT-pathway-altered" cohort; it enrolled a PIK3CAmut cohort. Including it in the AKT-pathway ODI artificially inflates the discordance toward 1.0. **Action:** either (a) report the AKT-pathway ODI on CAPItello-291 alone (it is a 1-trial token set, so ODI = 0 by definition, which honestly conveys "we have only one trial in this slot"), or (b) compute a cross-trial discordance only when ≥2 trials nominally target the same biomarker variable. The current 0.667 value is a comparison between a single AKT-pathway trial and a single PIK3CAmut trial and should not be presented as a within-variable heterogeneity statistic. The abstract (`main.tex:56`) quotes "AKT-pathway alteration (ODI 0.67)" as a headline finding — this number is an apples-to-oranges artefact.

15. **PIK3CAmut ODI does not differentiate kinase-domain-only assays (e.g. CAPItello-291's `helical_domain, kinase_domain`) from SOLAR-1's broader assay panel.** `06_compute_odi.py:39-42` gives SOLAR-1, INAVO120, and CAPItello-291 each their own token set. Of these, SOLAR-1's allowed both helical and kinase domains in tissue NGS; INAVO120 used a centrally-run tissue or plasma test; CAPItello-291 used plasma ctDNA only. The current tokenisation is reasonable but the absence of a `central_assay_required` token under-counts a real clinical discordance (FDA-approved companion-diagnostic test vs investigator-supplied report). **Action:** add `central_lab_required` to SOLAR-1 and INAVO120 sets, recompute.

## D. Framing and disclosure

16. **The 16-trial selection bias is now disclosed in `discussion.tex:25-26` but not in the Abstract or in the Limitations subsection of the Discussion explicitly.** The Discussion paragraph "Limitations" mentions "selection toward trials the guideline already chose to cite", but the Abstract (`main.tex:52-58`) and the Key Objective box (`main.tex:62-71`) describe the corpus as "16 pivotal phase 2/3 trials … spanning primary-completion years 2014–2024", without flagging that *all 16 were drawn from the ESMO/ASCO reference lists*. A guideline-reading clinician will infer that this corpus is the universe of HR+/HER2- mBC pivotal trials in that window, which it is not. **Action:** add to the Abstract a single clause: "…drawn from ESMO 2024 and ASCO 2024 reference lists (a pre-specified scoping choice; production-scale extension to all phase-2/3 HR+/HER2- mBC trials registered 2015-2026 is planned)."

17. **"Composition-only citations" framing in `discussion.tex:10-11` is now fair toward SOLAR-1 (G7) and CAPItello-291 (G6).** I accept the rephrasing: the manuscript no longer claims that these compositions are illegitimate, only that they should be made visible and counted. However the Abstract sentence "Evidence-free nodes under strict concordance clustered at post-CDK4/6i biomarker-stratified positions" (line 56) still uses the phrase "evidence-free" without the surrounding context — Round 1 ask #23 was to soften this to "compositionally-supported" or "non-directly-state-matched" in the headline language. The Abstract has not been changed. **Action:** edit the Abstract Results sentence to: "Five strict-concordance nodes were supported only by composition across non-overlapping pivotal trials rather than by a directly state-matched edge."

18. **The Methods §2.2 description of eligibility coding ("by hand-curated assignment from each trial's canonical primary publication and verified against the ClinicalTrials.gov eligibility text") is now contradicted by the four NCT mislabellings in §A above.** If the coding had been verified against the ClinicalTrials.gov eligibility text, the monarcHER/PALLAS/MONARCH-3/MONALEESA-7 swaps could not have survived: the eligibility text of NCT02513394 is unambiguously the adjuvant PALLAS protocol. **Action:** either correct the four NCT errors (asks 1–4) before claiming verification, or rephrase Methods §2.2 to "verified by trial-name lookup", which is a weaker but at least accurate claim.

## E. Remaining clinically-important pivotal trials whose absence weakens the EFDPR estimate

19. **EMBRACA (NCT01945775, talazoparib in gBRCAm HER2- mBC)** — see ask 10. Adding it strengthens G16 and gives a within-variable ODI for the gBRCAm operational definition (germline-only vs somatic, BRCA1 vs BRCA2 sub-strata).

20. **MONALEESA-3 (real NCT02422615, ribociclib + fulvestrant in postmenopausal HR+/HER2- mBC)** — see ask 3. ESMO cites it at G1 (1L postmenopausal) and G3 (post-endo). With this trial absent and the wrong NCT in its place, both nodes are encoded with an artificially small edge set.

21. **MONALEESA-7 (real NCT02278120, ribociclib + tamoxifen/NSAI + goserelin in pre/peri-menopausal HR+/HER2- mBC)** — see ask 4. Without it, G2 (1L pre-menopausal) becomes evidence-free, raising strict EFDPR.

22. **TROPiCS-02 dataset note (NCT03901339).** The Round-2 encoding `prior_state.post_chemo_metastatic_min: 2` is correct — the trial required at least 2 (and up to 4) prior chemotherapy lines for metastatic disease, plus prior endocrine therapy and prior CDK4/6i. The encoding is faithful. One small refinement: TROPiCS-02 also required *taxane exposure in any setting* (neoadjuvant, adjuvant, or metastatic), which is a non-trivial clinical constraint. Consider adding `prior_taxane_required: true` to the schema and the JSON for completeness. Headline-EFDPR impact is zero, but the schema becomes more honest.

23. **OlympiAD encoding (NCT02000622).** `02_extract_eligibility.py:179-187` encodes OlympiAD with `prior_state.post_endo: null, post_chemo_metastatic_min: 0` and `biomarker.hr_pos: null, her2_neg: true, brca_germline: true`. This is **clinically correct for the line-agnostic gBRCAm node**: OlympiAD enrolled both HR+ and TNBC (HR-negative) gBRCAm HER2- mBC, and the registrational analysis was an ITT of HR-mixed HER2- gBRCAm patients. The `hr_pos: null` and `post_endo: null` encodings correctly express the line- and HR-agnostic nature. Confirmed faithful — keep as is. (The drug-class label "PARPi (olaparib)" should arguably be normalised so that the G16 `recommended_classes` and the trial `drug_class` agree on a single PARPi token; right now `drug_class: "PARPi (olaparib)"` and G16's class `"PARPi (olaparib)"` happen to be identical strings, which is fine but fragile to a future EMBRACA add.)

24. **postMONARCH encoding (NCT05169567).** `02_extract_eligibility.py:150-158` encodes `post_endo: True, post_cdk46i: True, excluded.post_chemo_metastatic_any: True`, `drug_class: "CDK4/6i + fulvestrant (post-CDK4/6i)"`. **Clinically correct**: postMONARCH (Lilly's phase-3 abemaciclib + fulvestrant after prior CDK4/6i + ET) enrolled patients with progression on prior CDK4/6i + ET in mBC, with no prior chemo for advanced disease, HR+/HER2-. Confirmed faithful. The Round-1 fix is clean.

25. **INAVO120 encoding (NCT04191499).** `02_extract_eligibility.py:159-169` encodes `post_endo: True, post_cdk46i: False, endo_resistance_window_months: 12`, `excluded.post_cdk46i_metastatic: True`, `biomarker.pik3ca_mut: True`, `drug_class: "PI3Ki triplet (inavolisib + CDK4/6i + fulv)"`. **Clinically correct in substance** but the `endo_resistance_window_months: 12` token under-specifies what INAVO120 actually required: the criterion is "recurrence on or within 12 months of completing (neo)adjuvant ET, OR progression within 12 months of starting ET for metastatic disease". The schema currently captures the *window* but not the *anchor event* (adjuvant completion vs metastatic ET start). Round 1 ask #8 asked for a `primary_endocrine_resistance` boolean per ESO–ESMO criteria; this has not been added. **Action:** add `primary_endocrine_resistance: true` to INAVO120's schema entry to clearly distinguish the population from the broader "post-endo" pool, and report it as a methodologic refinement of the v1.0 schema for the v1.1 release.

## Summary

The four Round-1 corrections (postMONARCH, INAVO120, TROPiCS-02, OlympiAD) are themselves clinically clean and the framing of "composition-only" has been softened appropriately in the Discussion. However, Round 1 ask #3 — the systematic NCT-vs-name audit — was not performed, and the raw ClinicalTrials.gov payload exposes four further trial-identity errors among the 12 trials that Round 1 did not specifically scrutinise. One of these (monarcHER, NCT02675231, currently mislabelled as MONARCH-3) is in HER2-positive disease and must be removed; the other three (PALLAS-as-PALOMA-2, MONARCH-3-as-MONALEESA-3, MONARCH plus-as-MONALEESA-7) require NCT swaps and re-extraction. Two ESMO-encoding bugs persist (G10 still cites the wrong NCT in `03_encode_guideline.py`; G15 picks up a spurious SOLAR-1 match through a `_drug_match` prefix defect). EMBRACA is still missing from a node whose recommended classes explicitly include talazoparib. A regression test on NCT-vs-acronym identity would have caught most of this and should be added before v1.0.0.

The framework concept and the four Round-1-fixed edges are sound. The headline EFDPR will move once the four identity errors and two encoding bugs are corrected — possibly in either direction (correcting G6 to post-endo lowers strict EFDPR; removing monarcHER and adding the real MONALEESA-7 may raise it depending on G2's re-evaluation). The manuscript is closer to acceptance than in Round 1, but the persistent identity defects mean it is not yet a faithful pivotal-trial corpus.

**Verdict: MAJOR**
