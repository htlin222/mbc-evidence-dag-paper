## C. Faithfulness of the 16-node ESMO encoding

I read each node against ESMO 2024 (Annals of Oncology, Gennari et al. 2021 plus the 2024 update incorporating capivasertib and T-DXd). Spot-check findings:

3. **G6 (post-CDK4/6i, AKT-pathway → AKTi + fulvestrant) state encoding remains over-narrow.** Round-1 ask #14 and Round-2 ask #11 both noted that ESMO 2024's recommendation for capivasertib + fulvestrant in adult HR+/HER2- mBC with AKT-pathway alteration is anchored at "after recurrence on or progression with at least one prior endocrine therapy" — i.e. post-endocrine, with prior CDK4/6i permitted but not required. The CAPItello-291 registrational ITT is post-endo, mixed for CDK4/6i. The current G6 encoding `state: "post-CDK46i"` (`esmo_decision_tree.json:G6`) over-narrows the recommendation; ESMO does not say "must have had prior CDK4/6i". The discussion's framing ("CAPItello-291 enrolled a mixed pre/post-CDK4/6i population with only a subgroup readout", `discussion.tex:11`) defends the trial-level encoding but not the guideline-node encoding. **Ask 2:** either (a) recode G6's `state` to `"post-endo"` (which would move G6 from evidence-free to supported under strict tolerance, lowering strict EFDPR from 5/16 to 4/16 and making the headline numbers more honest), or (b) cite the specific ESMO 2024 paragraph that anchors the AKTi recommendation at post-CDK4/6i specifically (I do not believe such a paragraph exists; if the authors disagree they need to quote it). Without one of these two moves, an ESMO panel reviewer will flag the encoding as inventing a stricter requirement than ESMO does.

4. **G7 (post-CDK4/6i, PIK3CAmut → PI3Ki + fulvestrant) — BYLieve absence still not declared as a scope choice.** Round-1 ask #15 and Round-2 ask #12 noted that BYLieve (NCT03056755) is the trial ESMO 2024 actually cites for the post-CDK4/6i extension of alpelisib + fulvestrant. The manuscript continues to cite SOLAR-1 only for G7. The Round-2 reviewer suggested adding a one-sentence scope clause in Methods §2.1 excluding single-arm phase-2 expansion cohorts; this has not been added — Methods §2.1 still says "phase 2/3 interventional trials" without distinguishing single-arm phase-2 from comparative phase-3. As-written the reader cannot tell whether BYLieve was omitted deliberately or by oversight. **Ask 3:** add to Methods §2.1 a half-sentence scope clause: "single-arm phase-2 expansion cohorts (e.g. BYLieve) were excluded; the pilot is restricted to randomised comparative trials with a registrational primary endpoint" — and acknowledge BYLieve by name in Discussion §"Composition-only citations" as the clinically-relevant phase-2 trial that would convert G7 from evidence-free to supported if scope were broadened.

5. **G13 (post-CDK4/6i+post-endo, everolimus + exemestane) remains a separable node from G9.** Round-1 ask #16 flagged the G9/G13 overlap (a patient reaching G9 is by definition also post-endocrine, because CDK4/6i is administered with ET); Round-2 reviewer did not re-raise. The 16-node encoding preserves both nodes. Under the manuscript's own state-token logic, every G9-eligible patient also satisfies G13's state-superset. The continued separation is defensible only if the authors articulate the operational distinction in the supplement (Table S3 should record what differentiates G9 from G13 — e.g. "G9 = no further actionable target; G13 = separable post-endocrine progression event after CDK4/6i+ET"). Without that, the strict-EFDPR numerator includes one node that is arguably a duplicate of another, and a panel reviewer will read this as a counting artefact that inflates EFDPR by 1/16. **Ask 4 (MINOR):** add a one-sentence operational definition in Supplementary Table S3 (or in the JSON `notes` field on G9 and G13) clarifying the distinction; alternatively, merge the two nodes and re-run, reporting both versions transparently.

6. **G16 (gBRCAm → PARPi) — recommended-class column lists both olaparib and talazoparib at grade I-A, cited-trial column lists only OlympiAD.** This is the same finding as Round-2 ask #10. The supplement (`supplement.tex:126–129`) now acknowledges EMBRACA by name, attributes its absence to pilot scope, and commits to adding it in the production extension — that is adequate disclosure, and I accept it as a documented scope deviation rather than a blocker. **Ask 5 (verify-and-move-on):** confirm in the Discussion paragraph "Limitations" (`discussion.tex:25–26`) that EMBRACA's absence is reported there, not only in the supplement — the limitations paragraph currently mentions only the six NCT corrections, not the EMBRACA scope choice, and a reader who reads main text only will not encounter it.

7. **G3 (post-endo, CDK4/6i + fulvestrant) — only PALOMA-3 appears in `per_node_support` despite three cited trials.** Inspecting `efdpr.json:per_node_support.G3 = ["NCT01942135"]` — MONARCH-2 and MONALEESA-3 do not appear because their `year_pc` (2017, 2018) post-dates G3's `year: 2016`, failing the temporal-precedence check. This is the correct behaviour given the pre-registered temporal criterion, but it is clinically counter-intuitive: ESMO 2024 cites all three. The Discussion frames temporal precedence as if it never excludes supporting evidence; in fact it excludes two of three supporting trials at G3. **Ask 6:** in Discussion §"Algorithm transparency over clinical reasoning", add one sentence noting that the temporal-precedence criterion is applied against the *introduction-year* of each guideline node (`esmo_decision_tree.json:G3.year = 2016`), and that some clinically-supporting trials (MONARCH-2 2017, MONALEESA-3 2018) consequently fall outside the temporal window for G3 even though they are cited in the current ESMO 2024 update. This is a real consequence of the algorithm and the reader deserves to know about it.

## D. Framing: "conservative" vs "punitive" strict-tolerance

8. **The current framing of strict tolerance is balanced and adequate.** `discussion.tex:14` reads *"The strict concordance algorithm is deliberately conservative: it over-flags reasonable clinical extrapolations as evidence-free so that the resulting count is a robust upper bound on directly-supported decisions rather than a clinical judgement about extrapolation."* This is the right framing: "conservative" not "punitive", with the three pre-registered tolerance levels explicitly named as the mechanism for moving from upper-bound to extrapolation-permissive estimates. The Abstract (`main.tex:57`) describes the result as "best read as descriptive evidence of composition-across-non-overlapping-trials", which is appropriately measured. I accept this framing.

9. **"Evidence-free" terminology — bar-raise ask.** Round-1 ask #23 asked the authors to rename "evidence-free" to "compositionally-supported" or "non-directly-state-matched" in the headline language, reserving "evidence-free" for nodes lacking *any* trial edge under the liberal tolerance. The Round-2 reviewer pressed this point again (ask #17) for the Abstract. The current draft retains "evidence-free decision-point ratio" throughout: Abstract Methods (`main.tex:55`), Abstract Results (`main.tex:56`), Key Objective (`main.tex:67`), Introduction (`main.tex:81`), Methods (`main.tex:100`), Results (`main.tex:135`), and Discussion (`discussion.tex:5`, `discussion.tex:14`, `discussion.tex:23`). The phrase appears nine times across the manuscript. An ESMO panel reviewer would object to this label for the five flagged nodes (G5, G6, G7, G9, G13) on the following grounds: each of the five *is* supported by trial evidence that the guideline explicitly cites; what they lack is a *directly state-matched* edge under the framework's pre-registered state-superset criterion. To call them "evidence-free" reads, in clinical context, as if the recommendation has no empirical basis at all — which is not what the framework measures, as the Discussion now acknowledges. **Ask 7:** retain "EFDPR" as the named metric (the acronym is established and the supplement defines it precisely), but in the prose of the Abstract Results (`main.tex:56`) and the Key Objective Knowledge-Generated box (`main.tex:67`) replace "evidence-free nodes" with "non-directly-state-matched nodes" or "nodes supported only by composition across non-overlapping trials". The Discussion is already softer and can stand; the Abstract and Key Objective are the panel-facing headline and currently overstate the clinical implication. (This is the same ask Round 1 and Round 2 raised; in Round 3 I escalate it to a bar-raising find because the manuscript has otherwise reached publication quality and this is the one phrase that an ESMO panellist will not let pass without comment.)

10. **Would an ESMO panel object to "evidence-free" for the five flagged nodes specifically?** Yes for G5, G6, G7 (each is supported by a guideline-cited trial that fails strict state-match only on the prior-line definition: EMERALD on the ESR1mut subgroup readout for G5; CAPItello-291 on the mixed CDK4/6i population for G6; SOLAR-1 on the post-AI vs post-CDK4/6i window for G7). For G9 (post-CDK4/6i, no-actionable → everolimus or chemo), ESMO 2024 does not cite a single registrational trial; BOLERO-2 tested everolimus + exemestane post-AI (not post-CDK4/6i), and the recommendation is by extrapolation. "Evidence-free" reads acceptably for G9 — i.e. no panel member would defend the claim that any single pivotal trial supports G9 directly. For G13 (post-CDK4/6i+post-endo, everolimus + exemestane), the same BOLERO-2 extrapolation applies but the node itself is also operationally ambiguous (see ask 4). So three of the five (G5, G6, G7) would draw the strongest panel objection; two (G9, G13) would draw a milder objection that can be defused by the rephrasing in ask 7. **Ask 8 (related to ask 7):** in Discussion §"Composition-only citations" (`discussion.tex:10–11`), extend the explicit naming from SOLAR-1/G7 and CAPItello-291/G6 to include EMERALD/G5 (cited by ESMO for the registrational ESR1mut subgroup readout, which is exactly why liberal tolerance rescues G5 and strict does not). This makes the symmetry between G5/G6/G7 visible and converts the framing from "we flag five evidence-free nodes" to "we identify five nodes supported by composition or subgroup readout rather than by a directly state-matched edge — three of which (G5, G6, G7) are biomarker-stratified post-CDK4/6i positions and two of which (G9, G13) are extrapolations from BOLERO-2".

11. **Abstract clustering claim is mildly mis-stated.** `main.tex:56` reads "*Evidence-free nodes under strict concordance clustered at post-CDK4/6i biomarker-stratified positions (ESR1mut, AKT-pathway, PIK3CAmut, no-actionable-mutation, and the post-CDK4/6i+post-endo everolimus node).*" Of the five listed, four are post-CDK4/6i positions (G5, G6, G7, G9) but G13 (`state: "post-CDK46i+post-endo"`) is also a post-CDK4/6i position; meanwhile only G5 (ESR1mut), G6 (AKT-pathway), and G7 (PIK3CAmut) are *biomarker-stratified*. G9 ("no-actionable-mutation") and G13 (no biomarker) are not biomarker-stratified — they are line-of-therapy positions without a biomarker requirement. The claim "biomarker-stratified positions" therefore over-generalises from 3 of 5 to all 5. **Ask 9:** rephrase to "Five evidence-free nodes under strict concordance were post-CDK4/6i positions: three biomarker-stratified (ESR1mut, AKT-pathway, PIK3CAmut) and two without a biomarker requirement (no-actionable-mutation, and the post-CDK4/6i + post-endo everolimus node)." This is a one-line edit, but the current sentence is factually loose in a load-bearing position.

## E. 15-node sensitivity analysis

12. **The G16-removed 15-node sensitivity is adequate but the narrative needs one sentence.** `efdpr_sensitivity_no_g16.json` reports strict EFDPR 0.333 (Clopper-Pearson 0.118–0.616, exact $P=0.314$) and liberal EFDPR 0.267 ($P=0.539$). The Results sentence (`main.tex:172`) and Supplementary Text 1 (`supplement.tex:130–136`) report this clearly. The point estimate moves from 0.31 to 0.33 (because the numerator stays at 5 but the denominator drops from 16 to 15), and the qualitative conclusion (non-rejection of $H_0$, modest excess over 0.25) is preserved. **This is adequate reassurance that the G16 scope deviation does not drive the headline result.** I accept the sensitivity. **Ask 10 (MINOR):** add to the Results sentence one phrase noting that the sensitivity *moves the point estimate slightly upward* (0.31 → 0.33) when G16 is removed — i.e. G16 is the only supported node whose removal raises EFDPR. This is a small detail but it is exactly the asymmetry a sceptical reader will compute and the manuscript should pre-empt: the sensitivity does not "lower" EFDPR by trimming an evidence-free node; it raises it slightly by trimming a supported node, which strengthens rather than weakens the headline.

13. **EMBRACA's absence from G16 — adequately handled.** The supplement names EMBRACA, attributes its absence to pilot scope, commits to inclusion in the production extension, and the G16 `recommended_classes` lists both olaparib and talazoparib with the cited-trial column containing only OlympiAD. The drug-class equivalence table (`05_compute_efdpr.py:130–131`) treats `PARPi (olaparib)` and `PARPi (talazoparib)` as equivalent for `_drug_match`, so OlympiAD does support G16 under strict tolerance via its registrational PARPi class. **This is acceptable disclosure.** See ask 5 for the one remaining ask (mention EMBRACA in the Discussion limitations paragraph, not just in the supplement).
