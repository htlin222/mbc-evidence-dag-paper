# Editor's Decision Letter — Round 1

**Manuscript:** A Computable Map of Treatment-Sequencing Evidence in HR+/HER2- Metastatic Breast Cancer: Graph-Theoretic Discordance Between Trial Chains and Guideline Pathways
**Submitted as:** Original Report
**Handling editor:** (adversarial screen, JCO Clinical Cancer Informatics)
**Date:** 2026-05-16

---

## Editor's overall impression

The paper is technically well executed, pre-registered, and reproducible by construction — three things JCO CCI's editorial screen explicitly rewards. The arithmetic on the structure side (word count 2,260 main text; abstract 313; Key Objective block 119) is essentially in compliance, and the reference list (22 entries) is well under the 50-cap. What I am unwilling to wave through on the first pass is (a) the article-type self-classification, (b) the title's clinical-informatics framing, (c) the under-specified reproducibility statement, (d) the still-narrative "actionability" claim, (e) several author-metadata fields that block the manuscript-tracking system, and (f) the absence of the Supplementary checklist the manuscript itself promises. I am also asking for the cover letter and three suggested reviewers, neither of which are in the submission packet.

Below are 18 concrete asks. Address each one with either a tracked change or a point-by-point reply.

---

## Concrete asks

1. **Article type — re-pitch is the cleaner option, but Original Report is defensible.** The primary contribution is a method (the EFDPR/ODI framework and the (state, biomarker) DAG encoding) demonstrated on a 14-trial / 15-node pilot. JCO CCI publishes both Original Reports and stand-alone Methods Articles, and a 14-trial pilot with a 0.47-wide CI is a borderline-thin empirical demonstration to anchor an Original Report. Either (a) submit as **Methods Article**, narrowing the empirical scope claim to "framework demonstrated on a representative HR+/HER2- mBC pilot", and reserve the production-grade census for a follow-up Original Report, OR (b) keep the Original Report slot but explicitly justify the pilot size as the documented scope and add a section "Generalizability of the pilot" that frames the 14-trial corpus as the unit of demonstration, not the claim. State the chosen path in the cover letter. My editorial preference is (a); the current title is closer to (b).

2. **Title — re-frame for the JCO CCI readership.** "Graph-Theoretic Discordance Between Trial Chains and Guideline Pathways" reads like a methods-journal title (e.g., *Statistics in Medicine*, *JAMIA*). JCO CCI's accepted-paper titles lean clinical-finding-first ("X% of guideline decisions lack direct trial support in HR+/HER2- mBC: a computable audit"). Replace with a finding-first title under 150 characters; keep the method as the subtitle. Suggested: *"A Computable Audit of ESMO HR+/HER2- Metastatic Breast Cancer Guideline Decisions: Where Trial Evidence and Guideline Recommendations Diverge."*

3. **Word count is in compliance — leave headroom for revision.** Main text Intro + Methods + Results + Discussion is approximately **2,260 words**, comfortably under the 3,000-word Original Report cap. Abstract is **313 words**, **13 words over the 300-word cap** — trim the Methods sentence in the abstract. Key Objective / Knowledge Generated / Relevance block is **119 words**, just under the 120-word cap (no margin; any reviewer-driven addition will push it over). Tighten the Knowledge Generated paragraph by one sentence to create headroom.

4. **Reference list is under the 50-cap but materially incomplete.** Only 22 references, and the canonical primary publications a JCO CCI reader expects for HR+/HER2- mBC are missing or under-cited. Add (at minimum) primary references for: PALOMA-2 (Finn 2016 NEJM), MONALEESA-2/3/7 (Hortobagyi 2016, Slamon 2018, Tripathy 2018), MONARCH-3 (Goetz 2017), CAPItello-291 (currently has two duplicate Turner entries — `turner2023evidence` and `turner2023capitello291` resolve to the same paper, delete one), DESTINY-Breast06 (Bardia/Curigliano 2024), INAVO120 (Turner 2024 NEJM), postMONARCH (Kalinsky 2024 ASCO), EMBER-3 (Jhaveri 2024 NEJM), serena-6 / camizestrant if cited. The duplicate `turner2023evidence`/`turner2023capitello291` will be flagged by AMA-style copyediting; resolve before resubmission.

5. **Reference style is not yet AMA.** The template ships with `unsrtnat` + numbered superscript, which is close but not AMA 11th. At submission, post-process to JCO CCI's AMA style (period after author initials, no "and" before final author, journal NLM abbreviations, DOI as plain text not URL). The `cardosoESMO2024` BibTeX entry shows year 2021 / volume 32 — the ESMO 2024 update is *Annals of Oncology* 35:159–182 (2024) and the older 2021 paper is a different document. Fix the bibliographic record; this is the central guideline citation and the version-mismatch is a copyediting blocker.

6. **Reproducibility statement — make it actually reproducible.** The current Code Availability statement points to `https://github.com/htlin/mbc-evidence-dag-paper` and a "Zenodo DOI to be minted at release." JCO CCI requires a working DOI at the time of submission, not at release. Mint the Zenodo DOI now (tag `v1.0.0-submission` is the conventional move), pin the exact commit SHA in the Code Availability block, declare the Python version and the lockfile (`uv.lock` or `requirements.txt` with hashes), and add one explicit `make reproduce` target that regenerates Figures 1–3 and Tables 1–3 from a clean clone in under five minutes. Without a pinned DOI and a runnable one-line reproduction command, the reproducibility claim is hand-waving.

7. **Data availability — provide concrete identifiers.** Replace "Source datasets are public" with: (a) the exact ClinicalTrials.gov query string (URL-encoded, dated 2026-05-16), (b) the 14 NCT identifiers in a Supplementary Table, (c) the ESMO 2024 Annals of Oncology DOI, and (d) the SHA-256 of the frozen JSON corpus that ships in the repository. A JCO CCI reader running the pipeline three months from now must be able to verify they are operating on the same corpus.

8. **Clinical actionability — the "Relevance" paragraph is currently aspirational, not actionable.** Right now the paper says guideline committees and trial sponsors "can use" the EFDPR to prioritise. To clear the JCO CCI actionability bar, deliver one concrete artefact: a Supplementary Table (S5 or wherever) titled "Decision-node-targeted trial-design proposals", listing each of the seven evidence-free decision nodes with (i) the patient sub-state at that node, (ii) the candidate agents already in the corpus that *could* be re-positioned to cover that node, and (iii) a one-sentence trial-design sketch (sample-size order of magnitude, primary endpoint). Without this, the framework reads as a measurement instrument, not a guideline-committee deliverable.

9. **Reporting checklist — attach a concrete checklist as Supplementary Table S1.** TRIPOD is not a fit (no prediction model). The manuscript currently promises "a custom reporting-transparency checklist modelled on TRIPOD section structure" but does not ship it. My recommended choice is **PRISMA 2020 + a graph-construction reporting addendum**, because the analysis is structurally a structured-evidence-synthesis pipeline even though it is not a meta-analysis. Specifically: PRISMA 2020 items 5–24 (eligibility, information sources, search strategy, selection, data items, synthesis methods) map cleanly to your Methods. Add: a node-encoding subsection, an LLM-extraction validation subsection (per-field accuracy, Cohen's $\kappa$, prompt versions hashed), and a tolerance-tier definitions subsection. File as **Supplementary Table S1: PRISMA-2020 + Graph-Encoded Evidence Synthesis Reporting Addendum**. Cite the addendum in Methods. CHARMS-style charting and a SWIM-style narrative-synthesis adaptation are alternatives; pick one and commit.

10. **Author / affiliation metadata is incomplete.** The current author block reads "H.-T. Lin, Affiliation to be confirmed by author, ppoiu87@gmail.com." For an Original Report at JCO CCI this is a manuscript-tracking-system blocker. Required before resubmission: (a) full given names (not just initials), (b) a real institutional affiliation with country, (c) the corresponding-author ORCID iD (mandatory at JCO CCI submission for the corresponding author), (d) any co-author additions consistent with the CRediT block (currently sole-author, which is internally consistent but flags an editorial scrutiny step — confirm in the cover letter that no other person merits authorship under ICMJE criteria).

11. **CRediT statement — fine as written, but cross-check ICMJE.** The CRediT block names a single author across all roles. This is allowed but unusual for clinical-guideline-adjacent work. In the cover letter, affirm that (i) no clinician advisor, (ii) no statistical reviewer, and (iii) no LLM-extraction prompt engineer contributed at a level meeting ICMJE authorship criteria; if any did, add them. The Acknowledgements should name any non-author contributors and the LLM-extraction tooling explicitly (per JCO CCI's 2024 LLM-tooling disclosure norm).

12. **LLM-extraction methods are under-specified.** Methods §2.2 says trial-eligibility text was parsed "using a large-language-model (LLM) extraction pipeline with chain-of-thought prompts, two in-context exemplars, and per-field confidence labels", and §2.7 says the LLM-extraction validation paper "is reported separately." JCO CCI readers will not accept "reported separately" as a substitute. State, in Methods or in Supplementary Methods: (i) the specific model and version, (ii) the prompt template (versioned and hashed, shipped in the repo at a stable path), (iii) the hold-out validation N, per-field accuracy, and Cohen's $\kappa$, (iv) the human-adjudication protocol, (v) which fields were post-hoc human-corrected. Without this, the entire eligibility-structuring step is a black box and the concordance-algorithm conclusions cannot be audited.

13. **Pre-registration — provide a frozen, citable artefact.** Methods §2.7 says the pre-registration lives at `docs/prereg.md` in the repository and that the first commit hash records it. Promote this to a citable artefact: deposit a frozen copy on OSF (or the Zenodo DOI), report the deposition date, and include both URL and DOI in the Methods. JCO CCI's editorial screen treats GitHub-only pre-registration as fragile (history-rewriteable). The OSF/Zenodo timestamp is the audit-trail item the journal will look for.

14. **Sensitivity-analysis matrix is too thin.** The corpus-level result moves from EFDPR 0.467 (strict) to 0.400 (liberal), with the liberal lower CI touching the threshold. The paper currently treats this as robust; an adversarial reviewer will treat the liberal-tier touch as a result-fragility flag. Add: (a) a leave-one-trial-out sensitivity table, (b) a leave-one-decision-node-out sensitivity table, (c) an ASCO-decision-tree comparison (the manuscript pre-registers ASCO/NCCN sensitivity analyses but does not run them; either run them now or move them out of "pre-registered" into "future work"). The current Limitations paragraph admits the gap but the paper should not promise an analysis it did not deliver.

15. **Composition-only citations subsection (Results §3.4) should move earlier.** This is the paper's most interpretable clinical finding (SOLAR-1 cited for a post-CDK4/6i node despite enrolling post-AI; CAPItello-291 cited for an AKT-pathway node despite mixed prior-CDK4/6i). JCO CCI's house style is finding-first results subsections. Re-title §3.4 "Two ESMO-cited trials do not state-match the decision nodes they are cited for" and move it to §3.2. This single sentence is the cover-letter pitch.

16. **Figures — confirm vector format and Figure 1 legibility.** Figure 1 is a DAG with 14 edges and at minimum 7 source nodes; at 0.85\linewidth it has historically been illegible in JCO CCI's print column. Confirm the source PDF is vector (not raster), provide a high-resolution PNG fallback, and add Figure 1 alt-text (JCO CCI accessibility policy, 2024). All three figures together with three tables hit the 7-item combined cap — there is no room for a fourth figure without dropping a table.

17. **Cover letter — three sentences for editorial-screen pass-through.** Draft the cover letter `docs/cover_letter.md` and include these three sentences verbatim (one each, in this order): (i) *"This manuscript presents the first pre-registered, fully reproducible computational quantification of the structural concordance between the HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer trial corpus and the ESMO 2024 guideline decision tree."* (ii) *"The framework, the frozen schema, the ESMO decision-tree encoding, and the figure-regeneration pipeline are released open-source under MIT and archived under a Zenodo DOI at the tagged release, satisfying JCO CCI's reproducibility expectations."* (iii) *"Three actionable Supplementary deliverables — the PRISMA-2020+graph reporting addendum (S1), the decision-node-targeted trial-design proposal table (S5), and the LLM-extraction validation report (S6) — are provided so that guideline committees and trial sponsors can adopt the framework as a routine guideline-update audit instrument."*

18. **Suggested reviewers + non-overlap statement.** Submit three suggested reviewers (one mBC clinician, one biomedical-informatics methodologist, one guideline-methodology specialist), each with declared non-conflict and institution. Add an explicit non-overlap statement in the cover letter: "This manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere; an earlier version is/will be posted to medRxiv and the DOI will be supplied at acceptance."

---

## Verdict

**MAJOR REVISION.**

The science is sound and the reproducibility infrastructure is above the JCO CCI norm. The blockers are (a) the article-type mismatch between a method-first contribution and an Original Report empirical claim, (b) two metadata items (ORCID, affiliation) that the submission system will reject on intake, (c) the missing Supplementary checklist that the manuscript itself promises to deliver, (d) under-specified LLM-extraction methodology, and (e) the still-narrative "actionability" framing that needs to land as a concrete trial-design-proposal Supplementary Table. None of these are reasons to reject. All are reasons to revise before the manuscript reaches external review.

I would expect a competent revision in 4–6 weeks. On resubmission, the manuscript will go to two external reviewers (one clinical, one methods); the methods reviewer will probe items 6, 9, 12, 13, 14 hardest, and the clinical reviewer will probe items 2, 4, 8, 15. Prepare the rebuttal accordingly.
