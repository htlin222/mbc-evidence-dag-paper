# Round-1 Clinical Review

**Reviewer role.** Breast medical oncologist, ESMO mBC guideline panellist. Adversarial review of trial-eligibility encoding, ESMO decision-tree faithfulness, and clinical framing.

**Scope.** `manuscript/main.tex`, `manuscript/discussion.tex`, `analysis/02_extract_eligibility.py`, `analysis/03_encode_guideline.py`, `data/processed/trials_structured.json`, `data/processed/esmo_decision_tree.json`, raw ClinicalTrials.gov payload at `data/raw/ctgov_trials.json`.

The framework's claim is structural ("we count where guideline nodes lack a state-matched edge"). Because the count is small (n=15 nodes, n=14 trials), every individual encoding error directly moves the headline EFDPR. Below are the concrete asks the panel needs answered before this can be defensible as a clinical artefact.

---

## A. Trial-identity errors that change the headline number

These are not interpretive — the NCT IDs do not match the trial names the manuscript and JSON files claim.

1. **NCT03997123 is not postMONARCH.** `analysis/02_extract_eligibility.py:145` and `data/processed/trials_structured.json:275–296` label NCT03997123 as "postMONARCH" with drug class "CDK4/6i + fulvestrant (post-CDK4/6i)". The raw ClinicalTrials.gov payload (officialTitle, retrieved verbatim by the pipeline) is *"A Phase III Double-blind Randomised Study Assessing the Efficacy and Safety of Capivasertib/+Paclitaxel vs Placebo+Paclitaxel as First-line Treatment for Patients With Locally Advanced (Inoperable) or Metastatic TNBC"* — i.e. CAPItello-290 in **triple-negative** breast cancer, not HR+/HER2- mBC. The real postMONARCH is NCT05169567. As-encoded, the trial is both the wrong NCT and the wrong disease subset, and it is the sole edge supporting G10 (post-CDK4/6i all-comer CDK4/6i-switch). G10 should currently be coded as evidence-free, which changes the strict EFDPR. **Action:** either replace with NCT05169567 (and re-extract eligibility from that record) or remove G10 from the count and re-run the bootstrap.

2. **NCT04032080 is not INAVO120.** `analysis/02_extract_eligibility.py:153` and `data/processed/trials_structured.json:298–319` label NCT04032080 as "INAVO120" with drug class "PI3Ki + CDK4/6i + fulvestrant". The raw eligibility text begins *"Have a diagnosis of metastatic TNBC previously treated with standard anthracycline, cyclophosphamide, and taxane chemotherapy"* — this is LY3023414 + prexasertib in metastatic TNBC, not inavolisib + palbociclib + fulvestrant. The real INAVO120 is NCT04191499. This is the sole edge supporting G15 (post-CDK4/6i PIK3CAmut + endo-resistance triplet); as-encoded G15 is evidence-free and the "registrational triplet" framing collapses. **Action:** replace with NCT04191499 and re-extract.

3. **Verify every NCT–to–name binding in `STRUCTURED`.** Given two mismatches in 14 trials (14%), the burden is on the authors to demonstrate the other 12 are correct. A simple unit test that asserts `brief_title.lower()` contains the expected trial acronym (or asserts a hard-coded canonical PubMed-anchored mapping) should be added and the result reported.

## B. Prior-state encodings that are clinically wrong or misleading

4. **CAPItello-291 (NCT04305496, `trials_structured.json:97–119`) has `post_cdk46i: false`.** This codes the trial as *not* admitting prior CDK4/6i exposure, but CAPI permitted prior CDK4/6i and roughly 69% of the enrolled HR+ population had received one (per the NEJM 2023 primary publication). The correct code is "permitted, not required" — i.e. `post_cdk46i: null` with a note in the schema for "permitted_post_cdk46i: true". As-written, CAPI cannot match the post-CDK4/6i guideline node under any tolerance, which is the framework's point — but the *reason* the manuscript gives (Results §3.4, `main.tex:151–152`; Discussion `discussion.tex:13`) is "permitted but not required", which contradicts the JSON `false`. Fix the JSON or fix the text.

5. **SOLAR-1 (NCT02437318, `trials_structured.json:51–72`) has `post_cdk46i: false`.** SOLAR-1 *permitted* prior CDK4/6i exposure (the original protocol; small fraction enrolled), and the FDA label and ESMO citation distinguish the "post-AI" intent of the registration cohort from a hard exclusion. As-encoded, `excluded` contains only `post_chemo_metastatic_any: true`, so the JSON contradicts itself: `post_cdk46i: false` (not received) but no exclusion entry. The clinically faithful coding is `post_cdk46i: null` (mixed) with an annotated dominant population. **Action:** harmonise convention — does `false` mean "not received" or "not permitted"? The schema must make this unambiguous.

6. **DESTINY-Breast06 (NCT04494425, `trials_structured.json:121–143`) has `post_cdk46i: true`.** The raw eligibility text (verified in the API payload) requires *either* (a) progression within 6 months of 1L ET + CDK4/6i, *or* (b) ≥2 prior lines of ET ± targeted therapy in the metastatic setting. Prior CDK4/6i is therefore *not strictly required*. The enrolled population was ~90% post-CDK4/6i in practice, but the eligibility schema must reflect protocol-required, not observed, exposure. **Action:** recode as `post_cdk46i: null` (permitted/allowed via path-a only) and decide which guideline node it actually supports under strict tolerance. This likely moves G8 (post-CDK4/6i HER2-low) into the evidence-free set under strict tolerance, which the manuscript currently classifies as supported.

7. **DESTINY-Breast04 (NCT03734029, `trials_structured.json:253–273`).** The trial enrolled HR+ and TNBC; the HR+ cohort (n=494, ~88%) was the registrational basis for the HR+/HER2-low label. The current encoding (`hr_pos: true, her2_neg: true, subgroup_readouts: ["HR+_subgroup"]`) is internally consistent but the manuscript text in Methods (§2.2) and Results (§3.3, `main.tex:152`) should explicitly state that DB-04 is **registrational on the HR+ subgroup**, not on the all-comer ITT. This matters because the framework's "liberal tolerance" definition (accepting registrational subgroup readouts) hinges on this status; right now the abstract and §3.2 elide the difference.

8. **INAVO120 endocrine-resistance window.** Beyond the NCT mismatch, the conceptual encoding (`endo_resistance_window_months: 12`, `excluded.post_cdk46i_metastatic: true`) is itself slightly off for the actual INAVO120 (NCT04191499). The real INAVO120 requires *recurrence on or within 12 months of completing (neo)adjuvant ET, with no further therapy for metastatic disease*, i.e. a primary-endocrine-resistance population that is **CDK4/6i-naive in the metastatic setting** (CDK4/6i in (neo)adjuvant context after very recent label changes was allowed in a small subset). This is much narrower than "post-endo with a 12-month window" and is the entire reason INAVO120 reads as an early-line trial despite the PIK3CAmut biomarker. Please refactor the schema to capture "primary endocrine resistance per ESO–ESMO criteria" as its own boolean, or the framework's prior-state node taxonomy will keep mis-classifying primary-vs-secondary endocrine-resistance trials.

9. **MONARCH-2 (NCT02107703, `trials_structured.json:29–50`)** lists `excluded.post_chemo_metastatic_any: true`. The MONARCH-2 protocol excluded prior chemotherapy *for metastatic disease* but permitted prior (neo)adjuvant chemotherapy. The flag name `post_chemo_metastatic_any` is correct in intent but should be documented in the schema legend so that downstream readers don't conflate it with "no prior chemotherapy ever". A parallel comment applies to MONALEESA-2/3 and PALOMA-3.

10. **MONALEESA-7 (NCT02763566, `trials_structured.json:230–251`)** is encoded with `post_endo: false`. MONALEESA-7 actually allowed up to 1 prior line of endocrine therapy in the advanced-disease setting and included both tamoxifen and NSAI sub-arms. The `post_endo: false` flag is therefore inaccurate; the trial is a mixed first- and second-line endocrine population in pre/peri-menopausal women. As-coded it supports G1 and G2; under a stricter coding it supports both first-line *and* post-endo nodes, which would change the per-node trial counts in Figure 1 and the per-node strict-supp set.

11. **MONALEESA-3 (NCT02246621, `trials_structured.json:167–187`).** The encoding `post_endo: null, post_cdk46i: false, excluded: {post_cdk46i: true}` is internally contradictory with the use elsewhere of `null` for "mixed/permitted". Two strata were prespecified: 1L and 2L (post-endo) in postmenopausal women. The supporting-trial column for G1 (`esmo_decision_tree.json:G1`) does **not** list MONALEESA-3 but G3 (`esmo_decision_tree.json:G3`) does. ESMO actually cites MONALEESA-3 for both 1L (postmenopausal) and 2L. **Action:** add MONALEESA-3 to G1's `cited_trials` and document the dual-state encoding.

12. **MONARCH-3 brief_title is wrong.** `trials_structured.json:225` carries the brief title *"A Study of Abemaciclib (LY2835219) in Women With HR+, HER2+ Locally Advanced or Metastatic Breast Cancer"*. MONARCH-3 enrolled HR+, **HER2-negative** patients. Either the API payload is corrupt or the brief title is for a different abemaciclib study (monarcHER, NCT02675231 is in fact MONARCH-3 per CT.gov). Please re-pull the raw record and confirm — and add a regression test on brief-title-vs-encoded-disease.

## C. ESMO decision-tree encoding — node-level concerns

13. **G2 (first-line + pre-menopausal) is not a separate ESMO decision node from G1.** ESMO 2024 recommends CDK4/6i + ET (including LHRH agonist for pre/peri-menopausal) under a single first-line node with a sub-bullet on menopausal status, not as a parallel decision tree. Splitting G1/G2 inflates the denominator artificially and makes MONALEESA-7 do double duty. **Action:** merge G1 and G2 into one node with a menopausal-status attribute, or document why the framework's node taxonomy treats them as distinct (which it must do consistently for analogous sub-bullets elsewhere — see G14).

14. **G6 (post-CDK4/6i AKT-pathway → AKTi+fulv) and CAPItello-291.** The CAPI registration is *not* restricted to a post-CDK4/6i population — the FDA label and ESMO 2024 recommendation are for the AKT-pathway-altered population *post-endocrine* (with ~69% having had a CDK4/6i in practice). G6's `state: "post-CDK46i"` therefore over-narrows the actual ESMO recommendation. ESMO 2024 cites capivasertib at the "post-endocrine, AKT-pathway-altered" position, parallel to alpelisib at the PIK3CAmut position. **Action:** recode G6 with `state: "post-endo"` (not `"post-CDK46i"`) — which would also remove G6 from the evidence-free set and lower the strict EFDPR. If the authors keep `state: "post-CDK46i"`, they must cite the ESMO 2024 sub-section in the schema annotation, because as it reads now the encoding is inventing a stricter requirement than ESMO does.

15. **G7 (post-CDK4/6i PIK3CAmut → PI3Ki+fulv) is the most defensible "composition-only" example, but the grade is wrong.** `esmo_decision_tree.json:G7` lists grade "II-B"; ESMO 2024 actually grades alpelisib + fulvestrant in the post-CDK4/6i PIK3CAmut setting as "II, B" only because the post-CDK4/6i subgroup of SOLAR-1 was small and underpowered; BYLieve (NCT03056755) is the trial ESMO actually cites for the post-CDK4/6i extension. BYLieve is **missing entirely from the corpus**. Adding BYLieve would shift G7 from evidence-free to supported under strict tolerance and is the main reason a clinical reader will not accept the "post-CDK4/6i PIK3CAmut node is evidence-free" framing.

16. **G9 (post-CDK4/6i no-actionable → everolimus+exem or chemo) vs G13 (post-CDK4/6i + post-endo → everolimus+exem).** These two nodes describe overlapping populations. Patients reaching G9 are by construction also post-endocrine, because CDK4/6i is given with ET. Treating G13 as a separate, evidence-free node from G9 is an artefact of the encoding (a `+post-endo` qualifier added on top of `post-CDK46i`) and double-counts the same clinical decision. **Action:** either merge G9 and G13, or define the operational distinction in the schema (e.g. "post-CDK4/6i with separable post-endocrine progression event" vs "post-CDK4/6i no further actionable target"). Without that distinction, the 7/15 strict-EFDPR numerator is inflated by 1.

17. **G14 (post-2L → chemo or sacituzumab govitecan) cites no pivotal trial.** TROPiCS-02 (NCT03901339) is the registrational trial for sacituzumab govitecan in HR+/HER2- mBC and is the trial ESMO 2024 cites at this position. It is **missing from the corpus entirely**. Adding it converts G14 from evidence-free to supported under strict tolerance. The omission also means the manuscript currently claims "no included pivotal trial in our corpus targets this" (`esmo_decision_tree.json:G14` comment) without acknowledging that the omission is the authors' selection choice, not a property of the literature.

18. **Missing node: germline BRCA1/2-mutant → PARP inhibitor.** ESMO 2024 has a clear node for olaparib or talazoparib in gBRCAm HR+/HER2- mBC, cited to OlympiAD (NCT02000622) and EMBRACA (NCT01945775). Neither trial is in the corpus and the node is absent from the encoding. For an "ESMO HR+/HER2- mBC decision tree" claim, this is a load-bearing omission — gBRCAm is one of the four biomarker-stratified decision points in current practice (PIK3CA, ESR1, AKTpath, gBRCAm) and excluding it understates both the numerator and denominator of EFDPR. **Action:** add the gBRCA→PARPi node, the OlympiAD/EMBRACA edges, and re-run.

19. **Missing node: post-CDK4/6i HR+/HER2-low without endocrine sensitivity (DESTINY-Breast06 path-a vs path-b).** DB-06 has two eligibility paths (see ask 6) and ESMO 2024 cites it at two different positions: the immediate post-CDK4/6i HER2-low slot and the heavily-pretreated ET-only slot. The current G8/G11 split conflates these.

20. **G3 (post-endo all-comer CDK4/6i+fulv) is supported by PALOMA-3 / MONARCH-2 / MONALEESA-3.** This is correct, but the manuscript's framing of "post-endo CDK4/6i trials" as cleanly second-line is slightly misleading — PALOMA-3 enrolled patients with up to 1 prior chemo line for metastatic disease (the encoding `post_chemo_metastatic_min: 0` captures this, but the prose at `main.tex:152` does not). Please reflect in Methods that "post-endo" in the corpus is heterogeneous in chemotherapy exposure.

## D. Operational definitions

21. **HER2-low operational equivalence is overstated.** Section 3.3 (`main.tex:152`) describes DB-04 and DB-06 as "overlapping but not identical … main difference being DESTINY-Breast06's inclusion of HER2-ultralow." This is mostly right (DB-06 added HER2 IHC >0 <1+ as an exploratory cohort) but the *primary endpoint* of DB-06 was HER2-low (IHC 1+ or IHC 2+/ISH-), with HER2-ultralow as a prespecified secondary cohort. Please be precise: the registrational HER2-low definition is the same across DB-04 and DB-06 (IHC 1+ or IHC 2+/ISH-); the ultralow cohort is additional, not a redefinition. The ODI of 0.2 may be over-counting on this token.

22. **The "post-CDK4/6i" vs "post-CDK4/6i + post-endo" distinction (ask 16) is operationally meaningless.** Beyond G9/G13, the same conflation lurks in G5, G6, G7 — all of which describe patients who by definition received endocrine therapy together with CDK4/6i. Either drop the `+post-endo` modifier from G13 entirely (clinically it adds no information) or define a non-overlapping operational rule (e.g. "received an additional line of single-agent ET after CDK4/6i progression"). As-is, the manuscript's narrative that "the gap concentrates at post-CDK4/6i biomarker-stratified nodes" is partly a counting artefact.

## E. Framing and characterisation

23. **"Evidence-free" is rhetorically loaded for clinically-routine extrapolation.** The Abstract (`main.tex:56`), Key Objective (`main.tex:64–70`), and Discussion (`discussion.tex:6`) all use "evidence-free" to denote nodes without a strict-state-matched trial edge. Composition across non-overlapping pivotal trials is the *normal* mechanism by which oncology guidelines deal with combinatorial biomarker × line × class explosion; calling these compositions "evidence-free" implies the recommendation has no empirical basis at all, which is not what the framework measures. The Discussion §"Composition-only citations" partly walks this back, but the Abstract and Key Objective do not. **Action:** rename "evidence-free" to "compositionally-supported" or "non-directly-state-matched" in the headline language. Reserve "evidence-free" for the residual after composition is allowed (i.e. nodes with no edge under the liberal tolerance), which in this corpus is a different and probably smaller set.

24. **Drug-class naming.** Several recommended-class labels in `esmo_decision_tree.json` are sloppy in ways a guideline panel will flag: "SERD oral" (G5) should be "oral SERD (elacestrant)"; "AKTi + fulvestrant" (G6) should be "capivasertib + fulvestrant"; "PI3Ki + fulvestrant" (G4, G7) should specify "alpelisib + fulvestrant" because inavolisib (a separate α-isoform-specific PI3K inhibitor) is now also approved and is exactly what differentiates G15 from G4. As-is, the schema cannot distinguish two PI3K inhibitors with non-overlapping indications. **Action:** push specificity from class to drug-within-class; otherwise the "drug-match" criterion of the concordance algorithm (Methods §2.5(b)) is operationally ambiguous.

25. **HR+/HER2+ overlap is out of scope but should be acknowledged.** A non-trivial fraction of HR+ mBC is also HER2+. The decision-tree encoding silently restricts to HER2-negative and does not state how triple-positive disease is handled. Please add a Methods sentence ruling triple-positive disease out of scope (referring to a separate HER2+ guideline subset).

26. **Pre-registration claim needs a verifiable timestamp.** `main.tex:184` states that "the pre-registration commit hash is recorded in the repository's first commit." For an ESMO-panel reader, this is the load-bearing claim that the 0.25 threshold was not chosen post-hoc. Please cite the exact short SHA and date in the manuscript text and link to a public archive (the GitHub URL alone is insufficient if force-pushes are technically possible). A timestamped OSF or Zenodo registration is the conventional ask.

---

## Summary

The framework concept is sound and likely publishable in JCO CCI. The clinical execution, however, has two NCT-level data errors (postMONARCH, INAVO120) that change the headline EFDPR if corrected; at least three trial encodings that mis-state prior-CDK4/6i status (CAPItello-291, SOLAR-1, DESTINY-Breast06); a missing germline-BRCA decision node that any breast oncologist would flag immediately; an artefactual G9/G13 split that inflates the numerator; and a "evidence-free" framing that overstates the clinical implication of composition across trials.

None of this is fatal, but it is more than minor: the headline numbers will move, the panel-facing narrative will need rephrasing, and at least one entirely new branch (gBRCA → PARPi) must be added before the encoding can be called a faithful representation of ESMO 2024.

**Verdict: MAJOR**
