## Verdict rationale

The four Round 3 deferral items (R3-#7 bootstrap indexing, R3-#8 singleton-Jaccard limitation, R3-#5 modal-stratum disclosure, R2-#6 state-equality sensitivity) are documentation tightening that a JCO CCI reviewer will request at the journal revision stage; they are acceptable in a preprint with the cycle-history disclosure that the supplement already provides.

The six remaining items (asks #1â€“#6) are different. Three of them (#2 abstract trial-count phrasing, #3 OpenAlex non-use, #4 unpinned versions) are detectable from a first-page read of the preprint by any methods reader â€” a reader of just the abstract sees "16 + 1 = 17 trials"; a reader of Methods Â§2.7 sees library names without pins; a reader of Acknowledgements sees a data source that did not run. None requires re-running the primary analysis; all six are mechanical fixes totalling perhaps 30 lines of edits and four longtables generated from JSON.

A preprint that still contains a fourth-cycle prose overclaim ("parsed from the eligibility text" against a static dict), a populated-but-ignored data field across all 16 trial records, an unused preregistered data source in the Acknowledgements, an abstract that reads as a 17-trial corpus when the actual corpus is 16, unpinned dependency versions in the Code-Availability statement, and supplementary tables that are prose-substituted by JSON â€” is not ready for deposit on the strict reading that this final round was meant to enforce. None is a science-blocker; the science is correct. But the artefact does not meet the bar that Round 1, Round 2, and Round 3 collectively set for it.
