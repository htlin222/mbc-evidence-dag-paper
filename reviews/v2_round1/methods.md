# v2 Round 1 Methods Review

**Reviewer role:** adversarial methods reviewer
**Target journal:** JCO Clinical Cancer Informatics (Original Report)
**Manuscript:** `manuscript/main_v2.tex` + `manuscript/discussion_v2.tex`
**Materials reviewed:** `docs/prereg-v2.md` (incl. amendment v2.1), `analysis/v2_01_*` through `analysis/v2_11_*`, `analysis/extraction_protocol.md`, `data/processed/v2_*.json`, `data/results/v2_efdpr.json`, `v2_kappa.json`, `v2_kappa_postadj.json`, `v2_odi.json`, recent `git log` (commits `085ae54` → `9a12001`).

The pilot-to-production move is **substantively a real improvement** (systematic search, dual annotators, larger n, pre-specified subset sensitivity). The pre-registered headline test does reject H0 by a wide margin (P = 0.0002), and that finding is robust to the largest individual judgements (drop ESMO, drop ASCO, drop NCCN — primary still rejects on two of three subsets). However there are several concrete reproducibility, transparency, and pre-registration-fidelity defects that must be addressed before the paper can claim a pre-registered confirmatory result. The asks below are ordered by severity. Round 1 verdict at the end.

---

## 1. Pre-registration fidelity (must fix; blocking)

1. **ACTION: stop claiming the $\kappa \ge 0.70$ gate passes.** The prereg validation gate `docs/prereg-v2.md:95` says: "**LLM-extraction Cohen's $\kappa \ge 0.70$** on key fields (post_endo, post_cdk46i, biomarker requirements) — must pass before the full-corpus extraction is used for the primary test." The released data `data/results/v2_kappa.json` shows `passes_kappa_gate_07: false` (pre-adjudication: 4 of 8 key fields below 0.70: `post_endo` 0.40, `post_cdk46i` 0.67, `hr_pos` 0.64, `her2_neg` 0.65, `akt_path` 0.00). `data/results/v2_kappa_postadj.json` shows `passes_gate_07_post_adjudication_cohen: false` because `akt_path` remained at $\kappa=0.00$ post-adjudication. The manuscript Results §3.1 says "the pre-registered $\kappa \ge 0.70$ gate passes under PABAK on all key fields." **PABAK is not what the prereg specifies.** Either (a) acknowledge the gate failed and run the prereg's fail-path ("disclosed in the manuscript with a deviations paragraph and the analysis proceeds with the available data"), or (b) amend the prereg retroactively and disclose that. The current framing — substituting PABAK for Cohen's $\kappa$ after the data are seen — is a silent gate-swap and reviewers will read it as moving the goalposts.

2. **ACTION: disclose the n=30 vs n=25 deviation.** `docs/prereg-v2.md:65` step 7 commits to "expand to $n \ge 30$ guideline nodes for the primary test." The validation-gate section then states "Guideline-node count $\ge 25$". These two commitments are inconsistent inside the prereg itself; the delivered n=25 honours the gate but violates step 7. Either fix the prereg internal inconsistency (post-hoc disclosed) or add one explicit sentence to the manuscript Methods or Limitations: "v2 delivered n=25 against the prereg's step-7 commitment of n≥30; the n≥25 validation gate was honoured."

3. **ACTION: be honest about the order of operations for the systematic-search amendment.** The prereg v2.1 amendment paragraph (`docs/prereg-v2.md:107-112`) frames the amendment as "disclosed BEFORE LLM extraction." That is technically true. But the git log shows commit `085ae54` (prereg-v2) does NOT contain `analysis/v2_01_systematic_search.py` or `v2_02_filter_corpus.py`; those scripts first appear in commit `079b540` (the amendment commit), three minutes after the prereg, **with the amended thresholds already in place**. So the search script never existed in its pre-amendment form. The Methods paragraph `manuscript/main_v2.tex:84-85` says "The pre-registration (`docs/prereg-v2.md`) and its amendment v2.1 (commit `079b540`) were committed to the public repository before any v2 outcome-touching analysis." A skeptical reviewer reading the commit graph will conclude the amendment was decided in parallel with the first script run, not after a pre-committed pipeline ran and surprised the author. Add one sentence acknowledging that v2_01/v2_02 were committed at the amendment commit and that the pre-amendment thresholds (2015-2026, $\ge 50$) were never run end-to-end in committed form; the amended thresholds (2013-2026, $\ge 200$) are the only ones the public can audit.

4. **ACTION: report the by-source sensitivity tests with a multiplicity caveat.** Manuscript Results §3.4 reports ESMO-only P=0.027 (rejects), NCCN-citing P=0.020 (rejects), ASCO-citing P=0.057 (marginal). The prereg `docs/prereg-v2.md:69-74` lists these as **secondary** outcomes and applies BH $q<0.05$ only to S1 and S2 (ODI and lag), not to E1 (ESMO vs ASCO vs NCCN EFDPR), which it calls **exploratory**. The manuscript prose reads as if the three subset tests are confirmatory: "the primary result is therefore robust to dropping any single guideline source". State that the by-source decomposition is exploratory per prereg, that the three tests are massively non-independent (16+15+10 = 41 node-tests across 25 unique nodes), and that ASCO-citing P=0.057 is the more honest robustness signal than the pooled P=0.0002.

## 2. Concordance algorithm — code vs prose (must fix; blocking)

5. **ACTION: tighten the Methods prose around the state-superset rule.** Manuscript §2.6 says "every guideline-required prior-treatment token of $g$ is a member of the trial's source-state token set (with the special line-agnostic tokens `metastatic`, `indolent`, `visceral-crisis` satisfied by any state)". The code `v2_09_compute_efdpr.py:_state_match` strips three special tokens from the *guideline* side **but does not require any compensating match** — i.e., a guideline node with `state="first-line+visceral-crisis"` (G17) effectively becomes a guideline state of `first-line` only, and any first-line edge that matches drug class will satisfy G17. The prose says "satisfied by any state" but the implementation is "those tokens are simply deleted from the guideline-required state set before the subset test". Those are subtly different: a guideline node whose entire state is `metastatic` (e.g., G16 gBRCAm) reduces to the empty set, which is a subset of every edge — so the state criterion becomes vacuous, not "any-state". G16 happens to support NCT02000622 (OlympiAD) under this rule and would not under literal "first-line, post-endo, post-CDK4/6i" coding. Update the prose to match the code exactly, or change the code.

6. **ACTION: justify the line-agnostic special-token list.** Why these three tokens (`metastatic`, `indolent`, `visceral-crisis`) and not, e.g., `bone-only`, `pre-menopausal`, `post-menopausal`? The current set is just enough to make G16 (gBRCAm) and G17 (visceral-crisis) and G22/G24 (indolent) reachable without changing the trial extractions. A reader will wonder whether the special tokens were chosen after looking at which nodes were evidence-free, i.e., whether they are post-hoc relaxations to recover support. Add a one-paragraph justification with a citation, or run a sensitivity analysis with the special-token treatment turned off.

7. **ACTION: confirm or fix the SERENA-4/SERENA-6 NCT mix-up in the adjudication rationales.** `analysis/v2_06_adjudication.py:43-44` adjudicates `NCT04711252` with rationale "SERENA-6 (camizestrant + CDK4/6i): switch within first-line CDK4/6i after ESR1mut emergence". The extraction file `data/processed/v2_extraction_final.json` records `NCT04711252` as **SERENA-4** (1L camizestrant + palbociclib vs anastrozole + palbociclib; not a switch trial). SERENA-6 is `NCT04964934` per the same extraction file. The adjudicated `post_cdk46i: False` value is correct for SERENA-4 (no prior CDK4/6i) but for the **wrong reason** (the rationale describes a different trial). Either rewrite the rationale (likely the right action — SERENA-4 is 1L, no prior CDK4/6i is just "not post-CDK4/6i"), or, if the intent was to adjudicate SERENA-6, change the NCT ID. This is the exact same class of NCT-label bug round 1 of v1 flagged for the pilot; do not ship v2 with the same defect.

8. **ACTION: re-extract TROPION-Breast01 (NCT05104866) with its HER2-low subgroup readout, or explain why it is absent.** `NCT05104866`'s extraction has `her2_low: null` and `subgroup_readouts: []`. The trial's primary publication (Bardia et al. NEJM 2024) reports HER2-low as a pre-specified subgroup. NCCN G21 requires `HER2-low` and is flagged evidence-free strict; liberal tolerance does not rescue it because `HER2-low` is not in TROPION-Breast01's `subgroup_readouts`. If a HER2-low subgroup readout is added to NCT05104866, liberal-tolerance EFDPR drops by one node (G21 supported). This is potentially the single biggest single-trial extraction error in the corpus; verify against the canonical publication and re-run.

9. **ACTION: check the post-CDK46i node coverage for the registrational pivotal trials.** The strict-tolerance evidence-free set includes G13 (post-CDK46i + post-endo, HR+/HER2-, everolimus + exemestane, year 2018). The MONARCH-2 (NCT02107703) and SOLAR-1 (NCT02437318) and CAPItello-291 (NCT04305496) trials are all in-corpus and three of them are post-endo, so the algorithm correctly does not assign them to G13 (everolimus drug-class). But the manuscript Discussion paragraph "Three structural patterns dominate" should say plainly that G13's evidence-free status under strict reflects **drug-class non-match**, not **absence of a relevant trial population**: the post-endo + post-CDK46i population is heavily enrolled (postMONARCH NCT05169567, SPH4336 NCT05860465), they just test CDK4/6i + fulv, not everolimus. The "evidence-free" label can mislead a clinical reader into thinking no trial exists for that population.

## 3. Inter-annotator validation (must fix)

10. **ACTION: report the pre-adjudication and post-adjudication $\kappa$ side by side, in a table, with both Cohen's and PABAK, and label which fields started below the gate.** Currently the manuscript Results §3.1 reports a single sentence about pre-κ (0.40 to 1.00) and "post-adjudication Cohen's $\kappa$ ... 1.00 for seven of eight fields, with `akt_path` at 0.00 due to the well-known kappa paradox." That phrasing is technically correct but rhetorically minimises the failure: it suggests the only κ problem is the paradox on akt_path. In fact, 4 of 8 key fields (post_endo, post_cdk46i, hr_pos, her2_neg) failed the κ ≥ 0.70 gate **before** adjudication. The adjudication is a salvage operation, not a confirmation; it rewrites every disagreement to a "consensus" produced by Claude re-reading the protocol against itself. Add a transparent table:

    | field | pre-κ | post-κ | pre-PABAK | post-PABAK | gate (pre) |
    |---|---|---|---|---|---|
    | post_endo | 0.40 | 1.00 | 0.20 | 1.00 | **fail** |
    | post_cdk46i | 0.67 | 1.00 | 0.60 | 1.00 | **fail** |
    | hr_pos | 0.64 | 1.00 | 0.90 | 1.00 | **fail** |
    | her2_neg | 0.65 | 1.00 | 0.90 | 1.00 | **fail** |
    | her2_low | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | pass |
    | pik3ca_mut | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | pass |
    | esr1_mut | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | 1.00 | pass |
    | akt_path | 0.00 | 0.00 | 0.90 | 0.90 | **fail** (paradox) |

11. **ACTION: stop describing the adjudication as "Annotator A re-reads with the protocol". The committed `v2_06_adjudication.py` is not a two-annotator adjudication; it is a one-rater rewriting of both annotators' outputs to a single consensus authored by Claude alone.** Lines 100-108: `adjudicated_A = json.loads(json.dumps(claude[nct]))`; `adjudicated_B = json.loads(json.dumps(codex[nct]))`; then both are overwritten to the same value `val` from a hand-coded `ADJ` list. The post-adjudication $\kappa$ then compares `adjudicated_A` (= Claude with adjudication patches) to `adjudicated_B` (= Codex with the same patches), so by construction it is 1.00 on every field that had a disagreement. This is a circular validation: it does not measure inter-rater agreement, it measures whether the same patch set was applied to both arrays. The honest framing is: "post-adjudication, the two extractions agree by construction because the adjudication writes the consensus into both"; the post-κ is a sanity check, not a measurement. The genuine inter-rater quality of v2 is the pre-κ, which is 4/8 fields below the gate.

12. **ACTION: address the kappa paradox more carefully.** Manuscript §3.1: "`akt_path` at 0.00 due to the well-known kappa paradox (95\% raw agreement on a category with highly skewed marginals)." Quantify: out of 20 validation pairs, what was the akt_path TP/TN/FP/FN structure? If both annotators marked all 20 trials as `null` except one disagreement, this is a single discordant pair, not a paradox. With $n=20$ a single disagreement on a 95/5 marginal gives Cohen's κ = 0 by construction, but the inter-rater question for `akt_path` on a 20-trial sample is unanswered, not "well-known". Append a small confusion matrix and say what the disagreement was about and which value the adjudication chose.

## 4. Systematic search and corpus assembly

13. **ACTION: provide the 874 → 74 attrition tree with NCT IDs (PRISMA-style flow diagram).** Manuscript §2.2 reports "874 candidate studies; eight pre-specified filters; 74 systematic candidates". The committed `data/processed/filter_log.json` has the per-filter rejection counts but no breakdown of which NCT was rejected at which filter, and there is no rendered PRISMA. Spot-check three to five NCTs rejected at each filter (especially F5 her2_neg_pop, which uses a regex on titles + 6000 chars of eligibility) to give reviewers confidence that the filter is not silently excluding pivotal trials. The current claim "Six additional v1-cited foundational pivotal trials … failed the systematic filter for documented reasons" is asserted without a public attrition log per NCT.

14. **ACTION: justify the enrolment $\ge 200$ threshold in the amended filter.** The pre-amendment threshold was $\ge 50$; the amendment tightens to $\ge 200$. The justification given in the amendment is "to focus the corpus on pivotal-scale trials." But the date-widening direction was loosening (2015→2013 to recover PALOMA-3, MONALEESA-2, MONARCH-2); the enrolment direction is tightening. A reviewer will ask: why combine a loosening and a tightening into a single amendment? Were the v1 supplementary trials all $\ge 200$ enrolment? (PALOMA-2 = 666, OlympiAD = 302, SOLAR-1 = 572, EMERALD = 478, INAVO120 = 325, postMONARCH = 368 — yes, all $\ge 200$.) Then there is no harm; but state explicitly: "$\ge 200$ was chosen to align with phase-3 pivotal scale, and was verified compatible with the v1 supplementary set." A reader who imagines the threshold was chosen after observing which trials would be lost will be reassured.

15. **ACTION: state plainly that the v1 supplementary set was inserted by hand, not recovered by the systematic filter, and run a sensitivity EFDPR without it.** Six trials (PALOMA-2, OlympiAD, SOLAR-1, EMERALD, INAVO120, postMONARCH) were added by `v2_03_assemble_corpus.py`'s `V1_SUPPLEMENTARY` list. Five of these six are foundational evidence for G1/G3/G4/G5/G6/G10/G16 — without them, several of the supported nodes flip to evidence-free, which would push EFDPR up and the P-value down. Report a "systematic-only" sensitivity (74 trials, 16 nodes' worth of evidence stripped of these 6) so a reviewer can see how dependent the headline is on the supplementary set. Right now the headline `P = 0.0002` is on 80 trials including 6 trials that bypassed the filter.

16. **ACTION: reconcile the "80-trial / 64-edge" arithmetic.** Manuscript abstract: "80 phase 2/3 pivotal HR+/HER2- mBC trials … including 64 with canonical drug-class assignments". Manuscript §3.2: "Sixty-four trials had canonical drug-class assignments and entered the trial-DAG with concordance applied; 12 were flagged out-of-scope (HER2-positive, TNBC, adjuvant, or investigational-only) and 4 were investigational-class." The released extraction shows 12 trials flagged out-of-scope by notes AND 15 flagged `investigational (other)` by drug class, with **11-trial overlap**: union = 16, in-DAG = 64. The "12 + 4 = 16" framing in the manuscript implies disjoint sets; the actual decomposition is "12 oos (11 also investigational) + 4 investigational-but-in-scope = 16 excluded". Either state the overlap or restate as "16 excluded from concordance: 1 out-of-scope only, 11 out-of-scope and investigational, 4 investigational only."

## 5. Operationalization discordance (ODI)

17. **ACTION: justify why `prior_CDK4_6i` uses three tokens (required/excluded/permitted) and is computed across 80 trials while the other variables use 2-5 tokens and are computed across 2-9 trials.** The headline ODI = 0.64 on 80 trials / 3,160 pairs is driven by a three-state token space; with three equally-frequent states the Jaccard distance is asymptotically 1.0, so an ODI of 0.64 is a near-mathematical artefact of the encoding. The other ODI variables (HER2-low, ESR1mut, etc.) use richer token vocabularies that distinguish, e.g., plasma vs tissue. The cross-variable comparison "prior-CDK4/6i is the most operationally discordant" therefore conflates token-vocabulary granularity with actual operational heterogeneity. Either harmonise the vocabularies (e.g., also use 2-3 tokens for the others) or report ODI as variable-relative (not cross-variable rankable). The current Discussion paragraph "prior-CDK4/6i inclusion variable had ODI 0.64 — the most consequential single-variable heterogeneity" is at risk of over-claiming a methodological artefact.

18. **ACTION: clarify the ODI token assignment for ESR1mut.** `v2_10_compute_odi.py:53-60` assigns tokens by trial name (EMERALD/CAPItello-291 → `plasma_ctDNA, multi_variant`; PADA-1/SERENA-6 → `plasma_ctDNA, multi_variant, longitudinal_monitoring`; else → `tissue_NGS, plasma_ctDNA`). These tokens are hand-coded against the trial-name string, not parsed from the protocol or primary publication. The Methods §2.7 says "Token vocabularies are hand-curated from each trial's canonical primary publication"; the code shows the curation is at the trial-name level (4 named exceptions, everything else gets the default). State this honestly: "tokens are assigned per trial by the analyst against a small lookup table of named exceptions; trials not in the lookup table receive default tokens" and acknowledge that this introduces an investigator degree-of-freedom.

## 6. Other concerns

19. **ACTION: fix the Figure 1 caption: "G1--G26" implies 26 labels but the tree has 25 nodes (G18 was skipped during construction; see `v2_08_extend_guideline.py:45-52` which deliberately drops G18 as duplicate of G14). Either renumber to G1..G25 or note the skip.

20. **ACTION: clarify the post-adjudication Cohen's κ for `drug_class`.** The released `v2_kappa_postadj.json` shows post-κ = 1.00 for drug_class (because the adjudication overwrote both arrays). But the pre-κ was 0.83 with 85% agreement. The adjudication wrote three drug_class corrections (NCT01905592 → investigational (other); NCT04571437 → endocrine therapy alone; NCT05860465 → CDK4/6i + fulvestrant (post-CDK4/6i)). For these three, what were the two annotators' original values, and why did the adjudication choose the value it did? Without that detail, drug_class is a black box despite being the single most consequential field for concordance.

21. **ACTION: address the corpus-expansion HARKing concern explicitly.** The v1 pilot was 16 trials / 14 ESMO nodes / P=0.37 (fails to reject). v2 is 80 trials / 25 unified nodes / P=0.0002. The Discussion paragraph "Three caveats" handles the change as "consistent with statistical power from a larger n and a fully-disclosed corpus expansion, not with re-analysis of the same data." A skeptical reviewer will note: (a) the expansion was decided after the v1 P-value was known (the v1 result is in the prereg-v2 power-analysis table); (b) the expansion added both more trials AND more decision nodes AND a new statistical test (binomial vs CI-overlap); (c) the same author wrote both. The framing should be defensive but accurate: "v2 was pre-registered after v1 failed to reject; the expansion is in the direction of more power for the same hypothesis under the same operational definition; an independent confirmatory study would have to encode an independent guideline tree (NSCLC or mCRC) to be free of this concern." Acknowledging this is more credible than minimising it.

22. **ACTION: define "post-CDK46i biomarker-stratified" precisely.** The Discussion lists G5/G6/G7/G9/G13 as "post-CDK4/6i biomarker-stratified positions". But G6 is `post-endo` (not post-CDK46i) and G13 is `post-CDK46i+post-endo` (drug class everolimus, not biomarker-stratified). G19 (post-endo + ESR1mut) and G26 (post-CDK4/6i + AKTpath) are also biomarker-stratified post-CDK4/6i. The grouping is imprecise. Re-list the 15 evidence-free nodes with their precise state × biomarker × drug-class triples and group them by the operational reason for being evidence-free (drug-class mismatch, biomarker mismatch, temporal precedence, or genuine no-trial-in-corpus).

---

## VERDICT: MAJOR

The pre-registered primary test does reject H0 by a wide margin and the framework is fundamentally sound. The journal-blocking defects are (i) the $\kappa$ gate failed pre-registration and the manuscript substitutes PABAK without acknowledging this is a deviation; (ii) the "post-adjudication $\kappa$" is computed against the same array twice and is therefore mechanically 1.00 — it is not an inter-rater measurement; (iii) one adjudication rationale (NCT04711252) describes the wrong trial; (iv) the manuscript counts (80 / 64 / 12 / 4) do not add up because of unstated overlap; (v) one trial's HER2-low subgroup readout is omitted, causing a likely false-positive evidence-free node (G21) under liberal tolerance; (vi) ESMO/ASCO/NCCN by-source tests are reported as confirmatory when the prereg lists E1 as exploratory; (vii) the v2_01/v2_02 scripts were committed at the amendment commit, not the prereg commit, and the manuscript should not imply otherwise. Each is fixable; none is fatal; but together they make the v2 manuscript not yet acceptable as a confirmatory pre-registered study. Recommend a major revision with the 22 asks above, followed by a second methods round on the resubmission.
